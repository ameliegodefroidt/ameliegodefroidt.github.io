## Introduction

Statistics is more than numbers — it’s a powerful language for making better (business) decisions. In this course, we explore how data can be transformed into insight, and how statistical reasoning helps organizations understand uncertainty, identify patterns, and evaluate strategies.

Students will learn how to summarize and visualize data, draw conclusions from samples, and build and interpret statistical models commonly used in business contexts. Through hands-on exercises, interactive apps, and applied projects, students will develop the skills to analyze real-world (business) data and communicate their findings clearly and effectively.

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

* Apply key concepts of descriptive and inferential statistics to business problems.
* Use statistical software (SPSS) to explore, analyze, and interpret datasets.
* Critically evaluate data-driven claims in management, marketing, and finance.
* Present quantitative insights in a professional and data-literate way.

In sum, this course equips students with a practical toolkit to make evidence-based decisions in a data-driven world.
